A Brief History of Swaminarayan and His Contribution to Society

Swaminarayan, born as Ghanshyam Pande in 1781 in Chhapaiya (Uttar Pradesh), was a Hindu spiritual leader and the founder of the Swaminarayan Sampradaya, a reformist movement within Vaishnavism. At the age of 11, he left home as Neelkanth Varni and travelled across India for 7 years, promoting purity, devotion, and dharma. In 1800, he settled in Gujarat, where people were suffering from social problems, superstition, and violence. Swaminarayan worked to uplift society by stopping addictions, violence, animal sacrifice, and promoting education, morality, and equality for women and all communities.

In 1801, he was given the name Sahajanand Swami by his guru Ramanand Swami and later became leader of the fellowship. He established temples, taught people to follow strict moral living (like no alcohol, no meat, honesty, devotion to God), and wrote scriptures such as the Shikshapatri. His followers believe him to be a divine incarnation. The Swaminarayan Sampradaya grew rapidly in Gujarat and later across the world. Today, branches such as BAPS, Vadtal, and Ahmedabad diocese continue his teachings, running temples, schools, hospitals, and humanitarian work globally.
