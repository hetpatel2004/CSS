Swaminarayan Bhagwan — God

Brief Introduction

Swaminarayan Bhagwan, revered as Supreme by followers of the Swaminarayan Sampraday, is the founder of the faith; his teachings, scriptures and satpurush-guided way of life form the spiritual foundation of the sect.

Legacy & Key Teachings

  • He established the Sampraday and laid down spiritual guidelines for devotees.
  • He emphasized devotion, moral living, social upliftment, and worship — making temples (mandirs) the center of spiritual and community life.
  • He personally initiated worship in several temples and ordained that worship could continue under authorized acharyas / saints even after his departure. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}
  • Through the Sampraday, followers across regions found a unified path of devotion, ethical living, and community.

Major Temples (Mandirs) of the Sampraday

The following temples were constructed during the lifetime of Swaminarayan Bhagwan — these form the historic core of the Sampraday’s mandir-network. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

  • Ahmedabad (Kalupur) — Shri Nar Narayan Dev Mandir: The first temple of the Sampraday, constructed 1822; built from Burma-teak wood with detailed wood-carving; dedicated to Nar-Narayan Dev. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
  • Bhuj — Shri Nar Narayan Dev Mandir: Constructed 1823 at the request of devotees in Bhuj; worship of Nar-Narayan Dev; part of the original six temples established by the Lord. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}
  • Vadtal (Kheda district) — Shri Laxmi Narayan Dev Mandir: Built 1824; idols of Laxmi-Narayan Dev and Ranchhodji installed by Swaminarayan himself; also houses idols of Dharmadev, Bhaktimata, and Harikrishna Maharaj. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}
  • Dholera — Shri Madan Mohan Dev Mandir: Constructed 1826; another of the original temples; Lord Swaminarayan initiated worship here as well. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}
  • Junagadh — Shri Radha-Raman / Ranchhodrai Dev Mandir: Temple established by Swaminarayan in Gujarat — part of the early network of mandirs, with idols accordingly. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}
  • Gadhada — Shri Gopinathji Maharaj Mandir: Also among the original temples built under Lord’s instructions; part of the six original mandirs of the Sampraday. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

Purpose & Significance of These Mandirs

According to Sampraday history, these six temples were built so devotees could worship God in a fixed, sanctified place — to ensure the continuity of upasana (devotion) even after the Lord’s departure. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}

Wider Sampraday & Modern Mandirs

Over time, the Sampraday’s sphere expanded — new mandirs (both “Hari-mandirs” and “Shikharbaddha” type temples) were built by various branches and organizations under the Swaminarayan tradition, to serve devotees across Gujarat, India, and worldwide. :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}
